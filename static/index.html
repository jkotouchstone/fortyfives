Welcome to Forty‑Fives!

How to Play:

  1. Cards & Setup:
    Everyone gets 5 cards. Three extra cards (the kitty) are set aside.
  2. Bidding:
    Each player bids a number (15, 20, 25, or 30). You can see what others bid. If all pass, the dealer is forced to bid 15.
  3. Trump Suit:
    The winning bidder chooses a trump suit. Your cards remain visible so you remember what you have.
  4. Kitty & Discard:
    The bid winner adds the kitty to their hand and then picks their best 5 cards.
  5. Drawing:
    If you have fewer than 5 cards, you draw extra cards.
  6. Trick Play:
    In each trick, everyone plays one card face-up on the table. The winner (the highest card based on trump and following suit) gets 5 points and leads the next trick.
  7. Scoring:
    If the bidder doesn’t reach their bid, they lose that many points. The game continues until someone reaches 120 points.

Detailed Rules & Strategies for Forty‑Fives

Overview:
Forty‑Fives is a trick-taking game. You bid on how many points you think you can win, then play cards round by round. The player who wins a trick leads the next trick.

Bidding:
- If you're the dealer (in heads‑up), you can see your opponent’s bid (e.g., "Bill Passed" or "Bill bid 15").
- In middle positions, you see the previous bids before deciding your own.
- If everyone passes, the dealer is forced to bid 15 ("bagged").

Trump & Trick Play:
- The winning bidder selects a trump suit while still seeing your cards.
- All cards played in each trick are displayed face-up on the table for everyone to see.
- The Ace of Hearts is always trump.

Scoring & Score Sheet:
- Each trick is 5 points; the bonus trick adds 5 extra points.
- If the bidder fails to reach their bid, they lose that many points.
- When the game is over, a detailed score sheet shows each hand’s summary and the overall points.
