Detailed Rules & Strategies for Forty‑Fives

Overview:
Forty‑Fives is a trick‑taking game. You bid on how many points you can win and then play cards round by round. The winner of each trick leads the next.

Bidding:
- As the dealer, the computer’s bid is displayed immediately in the Game Notes (e.g., "Bill: bid 20" or "Bill: Passed").
- When you bid and win the bid, the status message will read, for example, "Player bids 20 and wins the bid. Please select the trump suit."

Trump & Trick Play:
- The winning bidder selects the trump suit while your cards remain visible in the “Your Cards” area.
- In trick play, each card played is shown in the Trick Area. If no trick is active, the last trick’s cards remain visible until the next trick.

Kitty & Draw Phases:
- If you win the bid, after trump selection you enter the Kitty phase, where your combined hand (your 5 cards + kitty) is displayed for selection.
- After confirming kitty selection, the Draw phase begins so that unselected cards are replaced until you have 5 cards.

Card Rankings:
- Trump order: 5, J, A, K, Q, 10, 9, 8, 7, 6, 4, 3, 2.
- Off‑trump order: K, Q, J, 10, 9, 8, 7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2, A.
- (Note: The Ace of Hearts is always trump.)

Won Tricks & Scoring:
- Each trick is worth 5 points (bonus trick adds 5 points).
- Won tricks are displayed as an "X" for each trick won. When someone reaches 120 points, a detailed score sheet is shown.
